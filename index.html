Life Story

Birth

The late Harison Kibuu was born in the year 1958 by his parents the late Muchiri Kabui and the late Monicah Wangui in Kimuri Village, Mwern Sub-Location, Giathugu Location, Mukurweini Sub-county, Nyeri County. He was the brother to Kabui Muchiri, Charles Waigera, Rose Waihtengeni Waigwa, Hannah Wairinun Ngure, the late Eunice Ndeti, the late Beatrice Wanja Munyiri and the late Rukenya Muchiri.

Childhood

The late Harison Kibuu was raised well by his parents, just like the other children of those days. He was an obedient and hardworking child who used to help his parents in various tasks and chores at home.

Education

The late Harison Kibuu started his education at Ngamwa Primary School in the year 1963 and later transferred to Mweru Primary School where he completed his primary education in the year 1971. He later proceeded to Kaheti Polytechnic and undertook a Masonry course and later went to Thika and studied an Electrical course.

Work

When the late Harison Kibuu completed his studies, he was employed in the Ministry of Public Work Mombasa where he worked until his retirement. After his retirement, he went back home and started farming. He was a successful Coffee and Livestock farmer.

Church

The late Harison Kibuu was baptized at P.C.E.A Ngamwa. He was later confirmed at P.C.E.A Ngamwa on 7/11/1982 by Rev. Eliphas N. Nkonge. He moved to P.C.E.A Kimuri where he was a member until his demise.

Marriage

The late Harison Kibuu met the love of his life, Alice Waruguru in the year 1985 and married through the Ugikuyu traditional marriage. They were blessed with children namely; John Gathumbi, Edwin Muchiri, Monicah Wangui and James Kabui.

Demise

On 1/12/2025 the late Harison Kibuu was involved in a road accident. He was rushed to Mukurweini Sub-county Hospital but passed away while receiving treatment. He died at the age of 67 years old. He has left behind his wife, children and grandchildren. IT IS THE GOD WHO GAVE US HARISON KIBUU AND IT IS HIM WHO HAS TAKEN HIM AWAY, THE NAME OF THE LORD BE PRAISED.
